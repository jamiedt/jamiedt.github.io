RICHARD MILLE'S ENDLESS SUMMER INTERACTIVE EXPERIENCE

Initial Experience

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

The very first thing I paid attention to when interacting with the experience was the amount of time it took for the experience to fully load in. There was text telling me what percentage of the experience had been loaded, and it was fading in and out. This fading in and out reassured me that the website was still working and that nothing had crashed, and the percentage was also constantly updated, showing me that progress was being made. The loading overall took 1-2 minutes, my guess being that it was loading all the images withing the experience so that it would run smoothly without any gaps or missing info. While it was loading I had the time to take in the art style as the loading screen was very colourful and interesting, although in the end I switched tabs while it was still loading because I had already taken in all the information on the page, and it was only halfway finished. One thing I found interesting was the fact that I needed to shorten my window from one of the sides to create a portrait view of the website. It felt a bit strange not having it take up my whole screen, but I found the interactive element of the sunrise going up as the window was shortened entertaining and thought it was a clever way to involve this technical asset towards the story of the experience. I’m assuming that the smaller window might be to make the experience more compatible between phone and laptop so that they didn’t need to create 2 different versions with different dimensions.

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

After spending two minutes with the experience, I created a list of each of my discrete actions in order.

1. Press Start
2. Click next on the tutorial
3. Used mouse drag to look around the screen
4. Scrolled the wheel to go to next scene
5. Clicked on the hidden feature
6. Scroll the wheel
7. Click on the poetry button
8. Scroll the wheel a few more times to finish the tutorial
9. Went through all the scenes searching around the scenes and clicking on any hidden objects as well as prompting the poetry with the corresponding animation.

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

When interacting with the experience I probably spent the most amount of my time searching around each individual scene as they were all beautifully illustrated and had many intricate details. I was also looking around to try and find the secret objects that were hidden around in the scenes which when clicked on, provokes a secret animation or feature. I found trying to find these hidden objects (that were outlined in a faint white line) very engaging and exciting for me and gave the website a deepened interactive experience.

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

My most common action when interacting with the experience was probably scrolling the wheel through the different scenes. The only way to move on and progress is to use the wheel at the bottom so the most common action I made was this because I wasn’t patient enough to read the poetry for every scene, and there were a lot of scenes.

The Goal

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

I believe the intended goal of the interactive experience is for marketing purposes. As much as it is an interactive piece of art, the experience ultimately ends by taking you to the Richard Mille website with their new summer collection of watches featuring the RM 07-01. The aim is to get viewers into the summery mood to have a greater chance of selling their product.

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience immerses the user in a serene and engaging summer atmosphere and evokes feelings of relaxation and joy to try and uplift the mood and get the viewer in a mindful state to possibly buy one of their watches. There are also subtle, underlying hints that Richard Mille uses to guide the viewer into the realm of their watches. The scrolling through the scenes imitates the cog wheels on a watch turning, the use of time throughout the story to depict the different scenes directly relates to the time on a watch, and the colours they incorporate are all found in their latest summer collection of watches which they advertise after, when you finish the experience.

User Interactions

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time? (For how long and how many different times)

My impression of the experience is that it is mostly a one-time experience as it is an exploration type of interaction, and the story doesn’t change once you have completed it. You can most definitely go back and complete it again if you rushed or missed things, but you will be repeating actions and going over the same content. Depending on how in depth you go into the experience, it can last from around 5 minutes, if you’re in a rush, to around 20 minutes, if you’re taking in all the information in every scene, but I personally think it gets a bit repetitive if you stay on it for too long.

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The experience encourages viewers to take it slow with many scenes that have a focus on relaxation like people doing yoga or a person relaxing in a hammock by the beach. The soundtrack has a slow BPM and plays calming music, advocating for a loose playthrough. At the end, the website also allows the user to share their experience, indicating that it is more of something that you complete, rather than something that you should come back to again and again.

What other media forms (digital or otherwise) does the experience reference?

The experience draws from virtual worlds, interactive art installations, and ambient sound design typically seen in high-end digital experiences or interactive websites. It also has a touch of the kind of sleek and deluxe aesthetic found in other luxury lifestyle brands' campaigns.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should act when engaging with your research experience?

The experience encourages a relaxed, open, and exploratory attitude. It's designed to be engaging and immersive, inviting you to spend time discovering its subtleties rather than rushing through it, with no clear objectives other than to enjoy the sensory journey.

What does this reference/s communicate to you about how you should feel when engaging with your research experience?

The design communicates that I should feel calm, intrigued, and inspired. The overall tone of the experience, coupled with the serene visuals and sounds, suggests a focus on enjoyment and mindfulness, which invites a more leisurely engagement.

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction to you and what makes that part frustrating?

For me the most frustrating part was the fact that I couldn’t have the experience in full screen. While it was a cool concept to make the viewer use a smaller window, it took a bit of immersion away from the experience as I had other tabs and my messy desktop in the background, distracting me from what I was doing. I would have felt a lot more satisfied if there was a way to full screen the website, even if the amount of interactive space stayed the same, for example, having the experience in the middle with just a flat colour or a gradient around the outside. /p>

My Final Feelings

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction to you and what makes that part satisfying?

For me the whole experience is extremely satisfying with feel-good elements ranging from the smooth scrolling of the wheel at the bottom of the screen to the beautiful animation and colour palettes. My personal favourite part was the little secret interactions and animations that you could find and click on as it gave me a sense of adventure and journey as well as a reward for finding the hidden element.
